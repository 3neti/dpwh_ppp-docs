# Draft Concession Agreement
**AI-Powered Independent Verification & Compliance Auditor (IVCA)**  
**Unsolicited Public-Private Partnership (PPP) Project**  
**Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH)**  
and  
**3NETI Research & Development OPC ("Proponent")**

---

## CONCESSION AGREEMENT

This Concession Agreement ("Agreement") is made and entered into by and between:

**DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC WORKS AND HIGHWAYS (DPWH)**

A national government agency of the Republic of the Philippines, with principal office at Bonifacio Drive, Port Area, Manila, represented herein by its Secretary,  
hereinafter referred to as the "Implementing Agency",

**- and -**

**3NETI RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT OPC,**

A Philippine-registered technology and AI company with principal office in Metro Manila, represented herein by its authorized signatory,  
hereinafter referred to as the "Concessionaire" or "Proponent".

Collectively referred to as the "Parties."

---

## RECITALS

**WHEREAS,** the Implementing Agency has determined the need for an AI-powered Independent Verification & Compliance Auditor (IVCA) to enhance procurement integrity, improve cost reasonableness, and strengthen oversight across DPWH procurement activities and infrastructure contracts;

**WHEREAS,** the Proponent submitted an Unsolicited Proposal under Republic Act No. 6957, as amended by Republic Act No. 7718 ("BOT Law"), offering to design, finance, develop, operate, and maintain the IVCA at zero cost to the Government;

**WHEREAS,** the Implementing Agency evaluated the proposal and granted the Proponent Original Proponent Status (OPS), recognizing the IVCA as a new concept and new technology under Section 4-A of RA 7718;

**WHEREAS,** the Project underwent (or will undergo) the required Swiss Challenge under RA 7718 and its IRR;

**WHEREAS,** the Government desires to formalize the arrangement through this Concession Agreement, establishing the rights and obligations of the Parties during the Concession Period;

**NOW THEREFORE,** for and in consideration of the foregoing premises, the mutual covenants herein contained, and the benefit that will accrue to both Parties, the Implementing Agency and the Concessionaire hereby agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I — DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings assigned to them:

- **"Project"** – The AI-Powered Independent Verification & Compliance Auditor (IVCA) PPP project as defined in the approved Feasibility Study and this Agreement.
- **"Concession"** – The exclusive right granted to the Concessionaire to implement, operate, and maintain the IVCA during the Concession Period.
- **"Verification Services"** – AI-enabled evaluation, integrity checks, cost reasonableness assessment, milestone validation, and compliance analytics for DPWH procurement and contract implementation.
- **"Contractor-Funded Fees"** – Fees collected from bidders or winning contractors, in accordance with the Fee Schedule approved by DPWH.
- **"Effective Date"** – The date this Agreement becomes legally binding following signing and fulfillment of conditions precedent.
- **"Concession Period"** – The duration during which the Concessionaire shall operate the Project.
- **"DPWH Data"** – Any data, documents, or digital materials provided by DPWH for Project operations.
- **"Milestone Verification"** – The process of validating percentage completion and deliverables using AI and related tools.
- **"Swiss Challenge"** – The competitive process required for unsolicited PPP projects.

---

## ARTICLE II — PROJECT SCOPE

### 2.1 Responsibilities of the Concessionaire

The Concessionaire shall:

1. Design, build, integrate, and deploy the IVCA platform.
2. Finance 100% of development, operations, maintenance, and upgrades.
3. Provide AI-assisted evaluation services, including:
   - Bid document analysis
   - Tender review
   - Cost reasonableness checks
   - Collusion/anomaly detection
   - Beneficial ownership verification
4. Develop and deploy AI-based contract monitoring systems, including:
   - Gantt chart generation
   - Slippage detection
   - Progress photo/video verification
   - Milestone-based payment validation
5. Provide audit-grade logs and evidence for DPWH and COA.
6. Ensure full compliance with:
   - RA 9184
   - RA 7718
   - Data Privacy Act
   - DICT Cybersecurity Framework
7. Provide training, capacity building, and support to DPWH personnel.
8. Collect and manage contractor-funded fees in accordance with Article VII.

### 2.2 Responsibilities of the Implementing Agency

DPWH shall:

1. Provide policy direction and oversight over the Concessionaire.
2. Review and approve outputs generated by the IVCA.
3. Facilitate access to procurement documents and authorized data sources.
4. Ensure all DPWH PMOs, BAC/TWG offices, and field units adopt the IVCA workflow.
5. Monitor compliance with KPIs and performance standards.
6. Exercise regulatory powers to ensure:
   - Data integrity
   - Transparency
   - Fair implementation

---

## ARTICLE III — CONCESSION PERIOD

### 3.1 Duration

The Concession Period shall be ten (10) to fifteen (15) years, beginning on the Effective Date, subject to final agreement based on the Feasibility Study.

### 3.2 Renewal

Renewal or extension is subject to mutual agreement and regulatory approvals.

---

## ARTICLE IV — DEVELOPMENT AND IMPLEMENTATION

### 4.1 Phases

The Project shall follow these phases:

1. System design and architecture
2. Prototype development
3. Sandbox integration with DPWH
4. Pilot testing in selected regions
5. Nationwide rollout
6. Optimization and continuous improvement

### 4.2 Standards

The Concessionaire shall comply with:
- National standards on cybersecurity (DICT)
- International standards (ISO 27001, NIST)
- Philippine government procurement and auditing standards

---

## ARTICLE V — OWNERSHIP AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

1. All DPWH Data remains property of DPWH.
2. The Concessionaire retains ownership of:
   - AI models
   - Software platforms
   - Proprietary algorithms
   - IP developed prior to the Agreement
3. Jointly developed materials may be governed by a separate DPA/IP agreement.

---

## ARTICLE VI — GOVERNMENT UNDERTAKINGS

DPWH shall:

1. Provide necessary cooperation and information.
2. Facilitate coordination with PMOs, BAC, TWG, and COA.
3. Endorse the IVCA workflow as part of official project evaluation.
4. Ensure that participation in the verification system is a required step for contractors.

DPWH shall not:
- Provide subsidies, support, or guarantees, nor
- Assume liability for private-sector financing arrangements.

---

## ARTICLE VII — FEES AND PAYMENT MECHANISM

### 7.1 Contractor-Funded Fees

The Concessionaire is authorized to collect:

1. Bid Evaluation Fee per bidder
2. Winner Verification Fee
3. Optional Contract Monitoring Fee
4. Add-on services (e.g., deeper verification, duplicity checks)

### 7.2 Fee Schedule

Full details are in the approved Fee_Schedule.md, incorporated by reference.

### 7.3 Government Financial Exposure

**None.**  
DPWH incurs no financial obligations.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — PERFORMANCE STANDARDS & KPIs

The Concessionaire shall meet the following KPIs:

1. System uptime: 99.5%
2. Procurement evaluation processing time: within 24–48 hours
3. Contract monitoring accuracy: ≥ 95% alignment with validated results
4. Response time for support: within 4 hours
5. Security compliance: Always evaluated "High" under DICT cybersecurity audits

Failure to meet KPIs may subject the Concessionaire to penalties.

---

## ARTICLE IX — REPORTING AND AUDIT

The Concessionaire shall submit:
- Monthly operational reports
- Quarterly performance reports
- Annual financial and compliance reports
- AI transparency reports

DPWH, COA, and PPP Center may conduct audits at any time.

---

## ARTICLE X — DATA PRIVACY & CYBERSECURITY

The Concessionaire shall adopt:
- Privacy-by-design
- Encryption (AES-256, SSL/TLS 1.3)
- Zero-trust architecture
- Role-based access control
- 24/7 security monitoring

All processing complies with RA 10173 (Data Privacy Act).

| Financial Risk | Concessionaire |

---

## ARTICLE XII — TERMINATION

Grounds for termination include:

1. Material breach
2. Failure to meet KPIs for two consecutive quarters
3. Fraud or gross negligence
4. National security concerns
5. Force majeure lasting more than 180 days

DPWH may assume control of operations upon termination.

---

## ARTICLE XIII — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Disputes shall be resolved via:

1. Good faith negotiation
2. Mediation under PPP Center rules
3. Arbitration under the Philippine Dispute Resolution Center (PDRCI)

---

## ARTICLE XIV — MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

1. Amendments must be in writing and mutually signed.
2. Notices shall be delivered to registered addresses.
3. Severability: If any provision becomes invalid, the rest remain in force.
4. Governing Law: Laws of the Republic of the Philippines.
