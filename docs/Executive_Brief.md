# AI-Powered Independent Verification & Compliance Auditor (IVCA) for DPWH
**Unsolicited Public-Private Partnership (PPP) Proposal**

---

## 1. Overview

The Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) manages one of the largest and most complex procurement portfolios in the Philippine government. With thousands of infrastructure projects across the country, ensuring fair bidding, cost reasonableness, technical accuracy, and timely project execution remains a continuous challenge.

The **AI-Powered Independent Verification & Compliance Auditor (IVCA)** is a zero-cost to government, PPP-driven solution that provides independent verification services to support DPWH procurement and contract management through artificial intelligence, contract analytics, and automated compliance auditing.

**Important:** IVCA is not a party to construction contracts between DPWH and contractors. All contractual obligations remain exclusively between DPWH and contractors. IVCA provides verification data and analytics to support decision-making, but all approvals and enforcement rest with DPWH.

The proponent, **3NETI Research & Development OPC**, proposes to build, operate, and maintain this system under a concession model, with contractors—not the government—shouldering the verification fees.

## 3. The Challenge DPWH Faces Today

Despite strong internal systems and dedicated personnel, DPWH remains vulnerable to:

### 3.1 Procurement Integrity Risks
- Bid tailoring
- Inflated cost assumptions
- Questionable eligibility documents
- Identity manipulation among contractors
- Collusion indicators across submissions

### 3.2 Evaluation Bottlenecks
- Thousands of pages of technical, financial, and legal documents
- Manual comparisons of unit costs, timelines, and specifications
- Difficulty ensuring uniform standards across regions

### 3.3 Contract Execution Risks
- Slippages and delayed detection
- Variation orders arising from poor initial validation
- Difficulty validating accomplishment reports
- "Ghost accomplishments" without geotagged evidence

Artificial intelligence provides DPWH a tool to detect these risks faster, more consistently, and more objectively.

## 4. What the IVCA System Does

The IVCA serves as DPWH's AI-enabled integrity layer across three stages:

---

### 4.1 Bid Document Creation Review

AI ensures that all bid documents are:
- Fair and non-restrictive
- Complete and compliant with RA 9184 templates
- Supported by reasonable cost estimates
- Free from ambiguous or discriminatory requirements

---

### 4.2 Bid Tender Evaluation

The IVCA analyzes all bid submissions using:
- NLP-based compliance checks
- Anomaly and similarity detection (against collusion)
- AI unit-cost reasonableness analysis
- Timeline and Gantt chart verification
- Beneficial ownership and identity validation

Outputs include TWG-ready analytics and risk scoring.

---

### 4.3 Contract Monitoring & Payment Verification Data

Upon award:
- AI generates contract implementation schedules
- Progress photos/videos undergo AI vision validation
- Milestones are verified and verification data provided to DPWH/COA
- Project slippage or risk is flagged early
- Gantt charts are updated dynamically

This provides DPWH with transparent, real-time verification insights. **All payment approvals remain the sole authority of DPWH and COA.**

### 5.2 Stronger Procurement Integrity

AI eliminates subjective interpretations and reduces opportunities for corruption.

### 5.3 Faster Bid Evaluation

AI assists TWG/BAC by reducing evaluation time from weeks to days or hours.

### 5.4 Lower Project Risk

Early detection of inflated costs, unrealistic timelines, or identity issues.

### 5.5 Enhanced Contract Delivery

Accurate AI-based milestone validation ensures payment is tied to real progress.

### 5.6 Better Public Trust

Transparency, auditability, and objectivity significantly improve public confidence.

## 6. Why a PPP? (Unsolicited Proposal)

### 6.1 No Fiscal Burden

DPWH pays nothing — cost recovery comes from:
- Small bid evaluation fees per bidder
- A verification fee from the winning contractor
- Optional AI-based monitoring fees

### 6.2 Long-Term Sustainability

The concession model ensures continuous updates, maintenance, and operations over 10–15 years.

### 6.3 Innovation Qualification

Under RA 7718 (BOT Law), unsolicited proposals are allowed when:
- They introduce new technology
- They require no government subsidy
- They deliver measurable efficiency gains

The IVCA meets all three conditions.

## 8. Proposed Concession Structure

**Term:**

10–15 years

**Investment:**

Privately funded (hardware, AI models, operations, compliance infrastructure)

**Revenue Source:**

Contractor-funded verification fees similar to SGS inspection models

**Government Obligation:**

None — no guarantee, no subsidy, no equity

**Oversight:**

DPWH retains full control over awards, decisions, and final approvals

**Transparency Mechanisms:**
- Fixed, pre-approved fee schedule
- Regular reporting
- Joint DPWH–Proponent monitoring committee
