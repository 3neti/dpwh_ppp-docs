# AI-Powered Independent Verification & Compliance Auditor (IVCA)
**Unsolicited PPP Project for the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH)**

---

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Background of the Project
- Context of DPWH's nationwide infrastructure mandate
- Current challenges in procurement integrity, evaluation speed, and contract monitoring
- Rationale for AI-enabled verification and compliance services

### 1.2 Objectives of the Feasibility Study
- To determine the technical, financial, economic, institutional, legal, and operational viability of the IVCA project
- To assess whether the project is suitable as an Unsolicited PPP under RA 7718
- To provide decision support for DPWH, NEDA ICC, PPP Center, and other oversight bodies

### 1.3 Scope and Limitations
- National scope across all applicable DPWH procurement and infrastructure contracts
- Focus on digital infrastructure and services, no civil works
- Limitations regarding data availability, historical baselines, and assumptions

### 1.4 Methodology
- Document review (RA 9184, RA 7718, DPWH manuals, COA circulars, etc.)
- Stakeholder consultations (DPWH, COA, PPP Center, contractors)
- Benchmarking against international practices (SGS, Bureau Veritas, similar govtech PPPs)
- Financial modeling and scenario analysis
- Risk assessment and sensitivity analysis

## 2. Project Description

### 2.1 Project Concept and Components
- AI-powered system for bid document review, tender evaluation, and contract monitoring
- Independent verification services, contractor-funded, under PPP
- Integration with existing DPWH workflows and systems

### 2.2 Project Objectives
- Enhance transparency and integrity in DPWH procurement
- Reduce cost overruns and delays
- Provide audit-grade evidence and analytics for oversight agencies

### 2.3 Project Outputs and Services
- Automated document quality checks
- Tender evaluation dashboards and risk scoring
- AI-generated Gantt charts and milestone schedules
- Milestone-based accomplishment validation
- Standardized reports for DPWH, NEDA, COA, PPP Center

### 2.4 Geographic and Sectoral Coverage
- National coverage across DPWH central office, regional offices, and select attached units
- Applicable to civil works, design-and-build, and other infrastructure modalities where DPWH is procuring entity

## 3. Demand, Need, and Problem Analysis

### 3.1 Current Procurement and Contract Management Processes
- Summary of existing DPWH procurement workflow (BAC, TWG, PMO)
- Current evaluation procedures for bids and contracts
- Current monitoring of project implementation and payments

### 3.2 Identified Pain Points
- Volume and complexity of documentation
- Manual, time-consuming evaluation
- Difficulty detecting collusion, bid tailoring, and overpricing
- Limited tools for real-time contract performance monitoring

### 3.3 Demand Drivers
- Increasing infrastructure investment targets
- Push for stronger anti-corruption and governance frameworks
- Growing expectations of transparency and digital government

### 3.4 Without-Project Scenario
- Continued pressure on human evaluators
- Higher risk of irregularities going undetected
- Persistent delays and cost overruns
- Limited auditability and slower COA reviews

### 3.5 With-Project Scenario
- AI-supported evaluation and monitoring
- Reduced evaluation time and increased consistency
- Better detection of anomalies and risks
- Improved project delivery and public trust
